## CRM System for Managing Leads

### Simple CRM Workflow (Using Spreadsheet Above + Tools)

#### Stage 1: LEAD CAPTURE

**Tools:**
- Google Sheets (Lead Pipeline tab)
- Google Forms (for inbound leads)
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (for research)
- Hunter.io / RocketReach (email finding)

**Process:**
1. Identify potential sponsor
2. Research contact information
3. Add to "Lead Pipeline" sheet
4. Set Status: "New"
5. Set Priority: High/Medium/Low
6. Schedule first contact date

---

#### Stage 2: OUTREACH

**Tools:**
- Email client (Gmail, Outlook)
- Email tracking (Streak, Boomerang, Mailtrack)
- Template library (Email Templates sheet)

**Process:**
1. Select appropriate email template
2. Personalize 20-30% of content
3. Send initial email
4. Update "First Contact Date"
5. Update Status: "Contacted"
6. Set "Next Follow-Up" date (3-4 days)
7. Track email open/click if possible

---

#### Stage 3: FOLLOW-UP

**Tools:**
- Calendar reminders (Google Calendar)
- Email tracking
- CRM sheet (follow-up alerts)

**Process:**
1. Check "Follow-Up Due Today" in dashboard
2. Send follow-up email (Follow-Up sequence)
3. Increment "Follow-Up Count"
4. Update "Last Contact Date"
5. Set next follow-up date
6. Move to "In Discussion" if reply received

**Follow-Up Cadence:**
- Day 0: Initial email
- Day 3-4: Follow-up #1
- Day 10-11: Follow-up #2
- Day 21-24: Follow-up #3 (final)
- Day 90: Circle back (if potential remains)

---

#### Stage 4: DISCUSSION & NEGOTIATION

**Tools:**
- Video call platform (Zoom, Google Meet)
- Proposal docs (Google Docs, Canva)
- Media kit (PDF)
- Contract templates

**Process:**
1. Update Status: "In Discussion"
2. Increase Probability to 30-50%
3. Schedule discovery call
4. Send media kit + proposal
5. Log all interactions in Notes
6. If pricing discussion: Update Status to "Negotiating"
7. Increase Probability to 60-80%

---

#### Stage 5: CLOSING

**Tools:**
- Contract templates (see above)
- E-signature (DocuSign, HelloSign, PandaDoc)
- Invoicing (Wave, FreshBooks, PayPal)

**Process:**
1. Send contract for signature
2. Update Contract Status: "Sent"
3. Follow up if not signed within 3-5 days
4. Once signed: Update Status: "Won"
5. Update Contract Status: "Signed"
6. Move to "Active Campaigns" sheet
7. Send first invoice (if applicable)
8. Send welcome email with next steps

---

#### Stage 6: CAMPAIGN EXECUTION

**Process:**
1. Track deliverable progress in Active Campaigns
2. Update brand regularly on progress
3. Submit drafts by deadline
4. Incorporate feedback
5. Publish content
6. Track performance
7. Submit metrics report
8. Invoice for final payment

---

#### Stage 7: RELATIONSHIP NURTURE

**Process:**
1. Send thank-you after campaign
2. Share final performance report
3. Ask for testimonial/case study permission
4. Add to email newsletter list (with permission)
5. Stay connected on social media
6. Check in quarterly
7. Propose new ideas for next campaign

## Personalization Formulas

### Framework: The 70/30 Rule

**70% Template | 30% Personalized**

Your emails should be:
- 70% reusable structure and core messaging
- 30% brand-specific research and personalization

This balances efficiency with authenticity.

---

### Personalization Checklist (Minimum Requirements)

Before sending ANY cold email, verify you've included:

‚òê **Contact first name** (not "Hi there" or "Hello team")
‚òê **Company/brand name** (at least 2x in email)
‚òê **Specific reference** to recent campaign, post, product, or initiative
‚òê **Audience alignment** - why YOUR audience matches THEIR target
‚òê **Unique value proposition** - what makes you different from other creators
‚òê **Specific deliverable ideas** - tailored to their brand needs
‚òê **Relevant results** - past work with similar brands/industries

---

### Personalization Research Process (5 minutes per brand)

**Step 1: Brand Research (2 min)**
- Visit their website homepage
- Note recent campaigns, initiatives, or announcements
- Check their "About" page for mission/values
- Identify current product launches or promotions

**Step 2: Social Media Review (2 min)**
- Check their Instagram (last 9-12 posts)
- Note content style, themes, hashtags
- Identify gaps or opportunities
- Screenshot posts that inspire your angle

**Step 3: Competitor Analysis (1 min)**
- Search "[BRAND_NAME] influencer partnerships"
- Look at what creator content they've shared/reposted
- Note style, format, messaging of successful collaborations

#### Formula 1: Recent Campaign Reference

**Template:**
"I came across [BRAND_NAME]'s [SPECIFIC_CAMPAIGN/POST] and was impressed by [SPECIFIC_DETAIL]."

**Examples:**

**Template:**

### Personalization Red Flags (Avoid These)

‚ùå **Generic compliments:** "I love your brand!"

‚ùå **Vague audience description:** "My followers would love this."

‚ùå **Copy-paste obvious:** Every email sounds identical
‚úÖ **Template with customization:** Core structure + 30% brand-specific details

‚ùå **No research evident:** "I think we'd be a good fit."
‚úÖ **Demonstrates knowledge:** "I noticed you partnered with [CREATOR] last month - my audience demographic is similar but skews 5 years older with higher household income."

‚ùå **Asking brand to educate you:** "Tell me about your products."
‚úÖ **Already informed:** "I've been following your [PRODUCT_LINE] launch and see strong alignment with my [CONTENT_NICHE]."

### Bulk Outreach Personalization Strategy

When sending 20+ emails in one session:

**Batch by Category:**
Group similar brands together and use category-specific templates with brand-specific customization.

**Time-Saving Process:**
1. Research all brands first (take notes in spreadsheet)
2. Draft all emails in one sitting using templates
3. Add personalization to each before sending
4. Space out sends (don't send 20 emails at 9:00 AM)

**Personalization Speed Tiers:**
- **Tier 1 (Dream brands):** 10-15 min research + custom email
- **Tier 2 (Strong fits):** 5 min research + template with 30% customization
- **Tier 3 (Volume outreach):** 2 min research + template with 20% customization

**Quality Control:**
Before sending, ask:
- "Would THEY know this email was written for THEM specifically?"
- "Have I referenced something specific to their brand?"
- "Is my value proposition tailored to their needs?"

If you answer "no" to any, add more personalization.

### Conversion Optimization

#### A/B Testing Framework

**What to Test:**

**Email Subject Lines:**
- Personal vs. professional tone
- Question vs. statement
- Short (<50 char) vs. medium (50-70 char)
- With/without emoji
- Name-drop vs. generic

**Email Body:**
- Short (150 words) vs. long (300+ words)
- Metrics-heavy vs. story-driven
- Direct ask vs. soft introduction
- Bullet points vs. paragraphs

**Follow-Up Timing:**
- Day 3 vs. Day 4 vs. Day 7
- Morning vs. afternoon
- Weekday vs. weekend

**Testing Process:**
1. Choose ONE variable to test
2. Send Version A to 25 brands
3. Send Version B to 25 brands
4. Compare response rates after 2 weeks
5. Use winning version going forward
6. Test next variable

---

#### Response Rate Improvement Tactics

**If Response Rate <5%:**
- ‚ùå Problem: Too generic, low targeting quality
- ‚úÖ Solution: Improve personalization, narrow target list

**If Response Rate 5-10%:**
- ‚ùå Problem: Good targeting, weak value proposition
- ‚úÖ Solution: Strengthen unique angle, add social proof

**If Response Rate 10-15%:**
- ‚ùå Problem: Strong emails, improve conversion
- ‚úÖ Solution: Optimize follow-ups, faster response time

**If Response Rate >15%:**
- ‚úÖ You're killing it! Focus on closing deals.

---

#### Win Rate Improvement Tactics

**If Win Rate <3%:**
- Issue: Not reaching decision-makers or poor fit
- Fix: Research better contacts, improve targeting

**If Win Rate 3-5%:**
- Issue: Getting conversations but not closing
- Fix: Improve proposals, media kit, negotiation skills

**If Win Rate 5-8%:**
- Issue: Good close rate, optimize volume
- Fix: Increase outreach quantity, build pipeline

**If Win Rate >8%:**
- Issue: May be underpricing or leaving money on table
- Fix: Raise rates, negotiate harder, test premium tiers
